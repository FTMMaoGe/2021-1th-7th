《切切乐》拍摄于2016年春天的上海，它讲述了一次平胸手术（又称双侧乳腺切除术，女性指派的性别多元者最常接受的性别肯定手术）的前前后后，采访手术当事人和朋友们，也与做手术的医生和护士对谈。一个不足以“变性”却又不仅是“美容”的手术，为何要做，如何去做，做完怎样？《切切乐》也许不是你遇到的第一部“跨性别”纪录片，但很有可能是最特别的一部。


A Fun Cut was filmed in Shanghai in the spring of 2016. Through dialogues with the “patient”, their friends, the doctor, and the nurse, it tells the story of a chest surgery (aka bilateral mastectomy, a gender-affirming surgery desired by many female-assigned gender-diverse people). Less than a “sex change” but more than a “face lift”, the film unveils the mystery surrounding this medical procedure. A Fun Cut may not be the first “transgender” documentary you have ever seen, but it is probably the most uncommon one.

你可以用任何方式来定义这段简短的影像。

给它贴上跨性别、易性病、平胸手术等标签。

或者，把它归类为自我探索、自我实现、身体政治、身体主权。

再或者，它就是一群人有说有笑地谈论着乳房，以及一个人有说有笑地切掉了乳房。

它没什么好遮掩，正视身体，穿衣的、裸露的、被切除的，一并呈现。

它也没什么要控诉或宣扬，直面困境，个人的、群体的、全社会的，举重若轻。

几个好友一拍即合，天南海北地奔赴现场，集体出镜加轮流掌镜，成就这趟几乎即兴的参与式拍摄。

这是一个开放的作品，是抛砖引玉，是对更多经验分享和理念交汇的邀请。

You may define this short film in whichever way you like.

Label it with transgender, gender identity disorder, chest surgery.

Or, classify it into self-exploration, self-realization, body politics, body rights.

Or just, it’s a group of people chatting and laughing about breasts, and someone chopping them off in chats and laughter.

It has nothing to hide; it looks at the body, with or without clothes, pre- or post-surgery.

Nor does it have anything to condemn of or advocate for; it embraces the predicaments of an individual, of a certain group, of the whole society, with humor.

Indeed, it’s a few friends who came together from across the country, without plotting beforehand, to act in front of and behind the camera, documenting in an impromptu and participatory style.

It’s a piece of open work, an invitation to sharing and communication.
